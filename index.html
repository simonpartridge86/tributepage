Bob Dylan's Bad Songs

Even the Nobel Laureate and "Voice of a Generation" has bad days at the office

Bob Dylan recording classic album "Bringing It All Back Home" in Jan, 1965. Most likely a good day at the office.

At Bob Dylan's Rock and Roll Hall of Fame induction ceremony in 1988, Bruce Springsteen exclaimed that "Dylan was a revolutionary – the way that Elvis freed your body, Bob freed your mind." While Bob's best loved songs and albums are certainly deserving of such praise, what happen's when old Zimmy isn't on his best artistic form?

Well, to remind you that even geniuses lack inspiration sometimes, here's a selection of Dylan's worst songs:

  • "Wiggle Wiggle" - Album: Under The Red Sky, 1990

    • You'll wiggle and squirm to the sound of these terrible lyrics, which were presumably improvised by a tired parent trying to entertain a bored toddler.

  • "Ballad in Plain D" - Album: Another Side of Bob Dylan, 1964

    • Plain is the operative word here, as Bob whines on about a recent break up at a glacial pace FOR EIGHT MINUTES!

  • "It Must Be Santa" - Album: Christmas In The Heart, 2009

    • AKA “It must be time for more eggnog!”, because you’ll certainly need to be a few sheets to the wind to get any joy out of Bob’s yuletide stinker.

  • "Floater (Too Much to Ask)" - Album: Love and Theft, 2001

    • The title sounds like an angry note pinned to an office toilet door. And frankly, I’d rather tackle drowning a coworker’s rogue no.2 than listen to any more of this s***.

  • "Joey" - Album: Desire, 1976

    • Just an 11-minute ode to the terrible and tragic death of a heroic… murderous gangster (?!?). The lyrics and motive might be morally questionable, but it’s the slow pace of this lengthy song that makes it irredeemable.

  • "Ugliest Girl in the World" - Album: Down in the Groove, 1988

    • The low-point of Dylan’s 80s rut, in which he presumably sought lyrical inspiration from the diary of an 8-year-old bully. Just awful.

  • "In Search of Little Sadie" - Album: Self Portrait, 1970

    • If you’ve ever wondered whether putting lyrics to the sound of someone tuning a guitar would make a good song, it's your lucky day. This song is in search of a melody and a purpose, and doesn’t find either of them.
